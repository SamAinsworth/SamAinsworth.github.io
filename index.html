About

I am a Research Consultant working in Computer Architecture, Microarchitectural Security and Microarchitectural Fault Resilience.

I'm also an Honorary Fellow at the University of Edinburgh, where I was previously a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in Systems and Hardware Security, and where I currently supervise Haocheng Xiao (Microarchitectural Security, with Nigel Topham). I formerly cosupervised Jackson Woodruff (now faculty at Edinburgh) with Michael O'Boyle.

Formerly, I was a Postdoc (2018-2020), PhD Student(2014-2018) and Undergrad (2011-2014) at the Computer Laboratory in Cambridge, where I currently collaborate with Postdoc Minli Liao (and former Postdoc Zhe Jiang, now faculty at Southeast University), and PhD students Mahwish Arif and Peter Zhang (all with Timothy M. Jones). I also supervise the Churchill College students in Computer Architecture, Security and Software Engineering, and Economics, Law and Ethics each year.

Research

My research interests currently include architectural and compiler techniques for:

  • Data prefetching, both in software and in hardware, particularly for irregular and big data workloads.
  • Efficient hardware fault detection and correction.
  • Systems, runtime and microarchitectural security.

Teaching

This gives students the opportunity to understand the ways real systems are attacked and defended, with a broad-church perspective from psychology and economics all the way down to computer architecture and transistors.
